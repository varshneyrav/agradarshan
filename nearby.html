Nearby Attraction

Prem Mandir

Prem Mandir in Vrindavan is all about Radha and Krishna's divine love. This beautiful temple attracts devotees and tourists alike from all over the world for its stunning architecture and spiritual significance. It is definitely a must-visit destination for seekers of tranquility and enlightenment.

Dwarkadhish Mandir

The Dwarkadheesh Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna as Dwarkadheesh, is an ancient and large temple near the Yamuna River in Mathura. Also known as Jagat Mandir or Nija Mandir, it is one of Mathura's oldest and largest temples.

Radha Rani Mandir

The Shri Radha Rani Temple in Mathura, India is a historic Hindu temple dedicated to goddess Radha. Perched atop Bhanugarh hills, 250 meters high, it venerates Radha Krishna as Shri Laadli Lal. The temple is a major attraction during festivals like Radhashtami and Lathmar Holi.

Birla Mandir

The Birla Mandir in Mathura, also known as the Gita Mandir, is a modern Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna. It features intricate marble carvings depicting scenes from the Bhagavad Gita and hosts cultural events, drawing devotees and visitors.

ISKCON Temple

The ISKCON Temple in Vrindavan, Sri Krishna Balaram Mandir, is a major ISKCON temple dedicated to Krishna and Balarama, with deities and festivals like Krishna Janmashtami and Gaura-purnima.

Banke Bihari Mandir

Banke Bihari Mandir is a famous Hindu temple in Vrindavan, India, dedicated to Lord Krishna. It was built in 1864 and is known for its unique idol of Krishna and traditions, such as closing the doors in the afternoon and not allowing devotees to see the idol's eyes directly.

Bateshwar Mandir

The Bateshwar Mandir is a temple complex located on the Yamuna River bank. It comprises 101 temples dedicated to Shiva, with a vibrant annual cattle fair and mela, attracting pilgrims from across India.

Chandrodaya Mandir

The Vrindavan Chandrodaya Mandir is a world-class and iconic monument dedicated to Sri Krishna in the holy land of Vrindavan. Once completed, it will be the world's tallest temple.

Gwalior Fort

The Gwalior Fort in Madhya Pradesh, India, is a historic stronghold atop a steep hill. Its diverse architectural ensemble including the Man Singh Palace and Teli-ka-Mandir, reflects the region's rich heritage. With breathtaking city views, it's a cherished tourist spot, embodying Gwalior's cultural legacy.

Nidhivan

Nidhivan is one of the sacred sites of Vrindavan, situated in the Mathura district, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is considered as the most prominent site dedicated to the pastimes of the Hindu deities Radha and Krishna and their cowherd companions, the Gopikas.

Radhavallabh Mandir

The Radhavallabh Lal Ji Temple in Vrindavan, India, is dedicated to Lord Krishna and was built in the 16th century. The temple is unique in that it does not have images or statues of Lord Krishna, instead, a flower or Tulsi leaf represents the deity on a simple throne.

Ghana Panchi Vihar

The Ghana Panchi Bihar in Bharatpur, India, is a national park with a unique bird feeding tradition and Siberian Crane migration. It's a Hindu pilgrimage destination during Janmashtami and Holi, but the number of Siberian Cranes has decreased due to habitat loss and hunting.
